# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Licensed real estate agents preparing a seller's home to list. They arrive from a
warm Curbio email (market-segmented, e.g. Atlanta). Context: skimming on a phone or
between showings, deciding whether Curbio is worth one conversation. The job to be
done is low-commitment: "show me what you'd recommend for my listing, and remind me
my seller pays nothing up front." Success = a free-prep-plan request (name + email,
phone optional).

## Product Purpose
Curbio is a pre-listing home improvement company. It renovates, paints, stages, and
repairs a home before it lists, and the seller pays at close rather than up front.
These landing pages are single-purpose conversion surfaces for email campaigns: one
offer, one form, no navigation escape routes. The page exists to convert warm
agent traffic into a contacted lead routed to the local Home Services Manager.

## Brand Personality
Luxury listing brochure, not SaaS. Editorial, calm, premium, confident. Voice is
short, plain, declarative fragments. Curbio is singular ("Curbio is", never "are").
Three words: assured, editorial, unhurried. The emotional goal is reassurance plus
quiet authority: an agent should feel Curbio is the established, licensed partner
that removes risk, not a startup making promises.

## Anti-references
- Generic SaaS landing pages: gradient heroes, hero-metric templates, identical
  three-up icon-card grids, purple accents, glassmorphism.
- Loud real-estate-tech: exclamation points, stock smiling-team photography,
  "transform your listing!" hype.
- Editorial-magazine cosplay: drop caps, italic display serifs, broadsheet grids.
  Curbio is editorial in restraint, not in affectation.

## Design Principles
1. The offer is the hero. "Your seller pays at close" is the single idea every
   section orbits. Say it plainly, repeat it without dressing it up.
2. One conversion action per screen. Warm traffic gets no menu, no secondary CTAs.
   The form (and one repeated amber pill) is the only thing to click.
3. Specific proof over claims. Named neighborhoods and real sold prices beat
   adjectives. Show the work; don't describe it.
4. Amber is a spice. Navy carries the page; amber marks exactly the next action and
   one or two emphasis words. If amber is doing work in three places in one view,
   remove two.
5. Calm by default. Motion is a single 8px rise on entrance, hover is color/shadow
   only. When in doubt, do less.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 on its background; large text ≥3:1. Inputs ≥16px to
prevent iOS focus zoom. Amber focus rings visible on keyboard, never a removed
outline. Tap targets ≥44px. Labels programmatically tied to inputs. All imagery has
descriptive alt text. Every entrance/transition has a `prefers-reduced-motion`
fallback (crossfade or instant).
